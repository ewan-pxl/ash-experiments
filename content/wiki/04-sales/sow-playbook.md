# The SOW and Proposal Playbook

How we turn a "how we'd approach" doc into scope plus a number a prospect can sign off on.

A SOW is the next step after a how-we'd-approach doc lands and the prospect wants to move. The shorthand doc carries no price. A SOW carries scope, timeline, and a number. The split is clean in Ash's own words: a "SoW with pricing" versus a "heuristics" doc. Once a doc has a dollar figure attached to defined deliverables, it is a SOW.

We ship SOWs as working drafts first, not signable contracts. The standard opener is a callout: "A working SOW for your review before we put a signable version in place. Numbers, scope, and timeline below, flag anything you want adjusted." The prospect reviews, we adjust, then we turn it into a signable version. The post-click team writes the scope and price; Graham reviews, makes it presentable, and sends.

## The three shapes of SOW
We run three distinct SOW shapes depending on the engagement. Pick the shape from what the engagement actually is, do not force one template onto all three.

### 1. Fixed-window project SOW (the SWAT-team build)
For a defined build or overhaul over a fixed window, not an open-ended retainer. The framing line: "This isn't a retainer. We come in as a SWAT team, hit clear deliverables across a defined window, and leave you with assets that compound." Green Garden is the reference: a 6-week onboarding sprint plus 3 months of execution, around 4.5 months end-to-end, at a flat monthly rate across the window.

Section recipe:
- **The setup.** One paragraph framing it as a fixed-window engagement, not a retainer. Name the sequencing if there is any (Green Garden: build Ferry Morse first, then port to McKenzie).
- **What you get.** Numbered, concrete deliverables. Green Garden: nav and cart overhaul; three landing pages each built as a standalone page and a meta object version the client can reuse without dev; 15 to 20 modular section blocks; a quiz funnel plus refreshed lead magnet.
- **What we're not doing.** The honesty section, same role as the "skip" section in a how-we'd-approach doc. Green Garden: no asset generation (we brief the client's illustrators), no second-brand build until the first ships.
- **Timeline.** Onboarding and execution broken out, with the end-to-end number.
- **Investment.** The monthly rate and the window it covers.
- **Who's in the room.** Named team with roles: Ash (Post-Click Director, owns the engagement), Lucas (Strategy), Ewan (Technical / development), plus a CRO design lead.
- **What we need from you.** Research access, brand assets, access to the client's designers, a recurring check-in slot.
- **Next steps.** Review, then signable SOW, then onboarding.

### 2. Two-route proposal SOW (build or replatform with options)
For a build or replatform where the prospect should choose how deep to go. Two routes at two prices on the same foundation. Bovita is the reference: Option A (Straightforward Migration, $16,850, 8 weeks) versus Option B (Full Discovery and Build, $28,300, 4 to 6 months), both onto Shopify on the same battle-tested UI kit.

Per-option recipe: Scope of work / What you get at the end / What this is not / Timeframe / Best fit if. Then a "How to choose between them" section that states plainly what the extra spend and time buy. Then "How we'd kick this off": holding deposit and onboarding form, agreed start date, technical discovery in the gap before start, payment terms (Bovita: 50% on start, 50% on completion).

### 3. Fixed-fee guarantee SOW (the performance bet)
For a single-deliverable engagement with a performance guarantee and a refund mechanic. The Artzabox template is the reference: a fixed-fee landing-page engagement to hit a 13.4% or greater uplift in average revenue per user (ARPU) versus the current page, measured in Intelligems.

Section recipe (this is the most contract-like shape):
- **Scope of Work.** The goal stated as a measurable benchmark.
- **Out of Scope.** Explicitly what stops (Artzabox: no split-testing once the uplift is hit; no ads, retention, or ongoing marketing).
- **Services Detailed.** Research and strategy, landing-page design and dev built native in Shopify, split testing of up to a set number of rounds using Intelligems as the source of truth.
- **Service Availability.** Timeline, working hours, communication channel and cadence.
- **Performance Metrics.** The success benchmark and the valid-test conditions (minimum traffic for significance, Intelligems validity thresholds, what makes a test invalid and not count toward the rounds).
- **Service Levels & Targets.** Round milestones (Round 1 launched within 21 business days of kickoff) and completion criteria (engagement ends when the uplift is hit or N valid rounds are done).
- **Client Responsibilities.** Access to Shopify, analytics, Intelligems and tracking; benchmark data; approving traffic splits.
- **Payment Terms.** Fixed fee, refund policy (Artzabox: refund most of the fee if the uplift is not hit after the agreed valid rounds, retaining a portion for research, design, and dev), payment schedule.

## How a deal moves
1. Pitch or teardown, then a how-we'd-approach doc (or audit) goes out. An intro call is the default but not mandatory if the prospect has already been pitched.
2. If they want to move, the post-click team writes the SOW: scope plus price. Reuse the closest prior contract as the base ("rip the other contracts for this").
3. Graham reviews, makes it presentable, sends it, often bundled with a quiz demo and one or two case studies.
4. Prospect reviews the working SOW and flags adjustments. We revise.
5. We turn it into a signable SOW. Onboarding kicks off (see `03-delivery/`).
