# The Brand Audit Method

How we research a prospect before a call so the conversation is diagnostic, not generic.

The brand audit is the prep work that sits behind a strong pitch. It is a full-funnel research pass on a prospect that produces three things we can use on a call: a snapshot of who they are, three pain points framed as diagnoses, and three growth plays mapped to those pains. It exists so we walk into the call already understanding the brand, with specific observations and the questions we want answered, rather than reacting live. The Notion template (`356b4e6a-dca1-8146-a6ad-eba33f9ff15a`) is the working tool; duplicate it per prospect and rename to "[Brand] Brand Audit ([Month YYYY])".

This is the input. A how-we'd-approach doc (see `how-we-d-approach.md`) is what we send the prospect afterwards. The audit is internal-facing and diagnostic; the doc is client-facing and positioning.

## The shape: snapshot, then research, then diagnose, then prescribe
Work top to bottom. Each stage feeds the next. Skipping the research stage leaves blind spots on the call.

### 1. Brand snapshot
Fill this first. It is the 30-second context lens for everything below: brand, category and sub-category, founders and leadership, estimated revenue band, stage and maturity signals (funding, headcount, retail, awards, press), the one-sentence positioning, who the audit is being prepped for (role plus name), and the likely engagement fit (retainer, growth partnership, paid audit, or to-be-decided).

### 2. Research checklist (full-funnel)
Tick these before drafting pain points. Organised by funnel stage:

- **Awareness / top-of-funnel.** Meta Ad Library (active ad count, creative types, hooks, percent video, ad longevity), TikTok presence, Instagram (followers, cadence, content mix, engagement), PR hits over the last 12 months, influencer and creator presence.
- **Acquisition.** Paid landing pages (advertorial, product page, collection, quiz), offer structure (first-purchase incentive, bundles, subscription, free-shipping threshold), SEO snapshot (brand vs non-brand traffic mix via SimilarWeb or Ahrefs, top organic keywords, blog cadence), visible affiliate or partnership.
- **Conversion.** Homepage (above-fold value prop, social proof, hero CTA), collection pages (filtering, merchandising), product pages (imagery, sizing and fit content, reviews placement, urgency, cross-sell), cart (upsell logic, free-shipping bar, trust badges), checkout (field count, express pay, mobile UX), full mobile experience (over 70% of traffic for most DTC brands), site speed (PageSpeed Insights, mobile).
- **Retention / lifecycle.** Signup capture (incentive, fields, exit-intent), welcome flow (trigger an email and screenshot the sequence), abandonment flows (cart, browse, checkout, do all three exist), post-purchase (thank-you, review request, cross-sell, replenishment), SMS, subscription or loyalty program.
- **Tech stack and data.** BuiltWith or Wappalyzer (platform, email service provider, reviews tool, analytics, CRO tools), customer reviews across Trustpilot, BBB, Reddit, app stores (specifically: what do they complain about).

### 3. Three pain points
Diagnostic tone. Lead with the observation, then the hypothesis, then the question for the call. Each pain point carries: what we observed, why we think it matters, the likely impact (pick one of CAC, AOV, return rate, LTV), and the question we will ask on the call. Three is the number, not more. The point is to surface a manageable set of high-conviction problems, each one earning a reaction from the prospect.

### 4. Three growth plays
Each play maps back to at least one pain point. Lead with the play, then the mechanic, then how Pixel Theory executes it. Each play carries: the play in one line, why it works for this brand, the mechanic for the first 30 days, how Pixel Theory runs it, and what success looks like in 90 days.

### 5. Suggested next step
The cleanest path from this call to a signed engagement. Pick one primary recommendation (for example, a 4-week sprint at a set price to validate one play before a broader retainer), state why this first rather than the full partnership, what we'd need from them to start, and what we'd deliver in the first 30 days.

### 6. Talking-track for the call
The live flow, kept to 30 minutes: 0 to 3 minutes intro and confirm what they want from the call, 3 to 8 minutes the snapshot ("here's what we read about you"), 8 to 18 minutes the three pain points with a pause for reaction on each, 18 to 26 minutes the three growth plays, 26 to 30 minutes the next step and close.

### 7. Sources / appendix
Drop links and screenshots as they come in. The audit is only as credible as the evidence behind each observation.

## How the audit feeds the rest of the motion
- The **snapshot** feeds the opener of a how-we'd-approach doc (the "isn't a brand that needs X" line comes from genuinely understanding their stage and what is already strong).
- The **pain points** become the constraint framing in section 1 of the doc.
- The **growth plays** sort into the ops-driven and tag-on buckets of section 2.
- The **research checklist gaps** become the "what we'd need from you" asks in section 3, because anything we could not see from the outside is something we need access or data to confirm.
- The **suggested next step** is the bridge to a SOW (see `sow-playbook.md`).

The audit can also be used as a sales lever in its own right: a rough one-to-two pages on what is wrong with a site plus a page of tests they should run, sent to pull a prospect deeper into the process. In a pitch, mocking up one or two components in the prospect's own brand style (a listicle or quiz that looks like their brand) makes the audit land harder.
