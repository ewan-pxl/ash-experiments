# The Post-Click Offering

What we sell, at what price, and how we place a brand into the right tier.

Post-click is everything after the click. Paid media gets the visitor to the site; post-click
turns that visitor into a customer. We design, build, and test the landing pages, funnels, and
on-site experiences that convert paid traffic, and we treat it as part of the growth engine,
not a separate service. Every tier runs on the same sprint rhythm and the same testing
discipline. What changes across tiers is the sprint cadence, the build surface area, and how
much of the Shopify back-end and creative we own.

**On "tests or iterations":** each sprint carries a fixed number of test slots that flex
between isolated A/B tests and landing page iterations (same page, nuanced copy or asset
changes per angle or persona). It is the same number either way. That flex is what lets a
sprint support in-platform angle testing as well as on-site split testing.

## How we place a brand into a tier

Owned by the sales director (Graham). Three signals matter most: monthly sessions, monthly
revenue, and monthly ad spend. The strongest signal is revenue and ad spend together. A
high-revenue brand with growing paid spend almost always belongs in Growth or Turnkey,
regardless of session count.

**The minimum floor.** Below 25,000 monthly sessions or $75,000 monthly revenue, structured
testing cannot resolve cleanly within sprint windows and the ROI does not justify the
retainer. Below the floor, steer the brand to heuristic CRO and revisit once they are
consistently above it, ideally with a focused SKU range.

**The store complexity multiplier.** Two brands at the same sessions can have very different
testability. A focused line concentrates traffic onto the pages we test; a complex catalog
fragments it. One product with 3 variants at 50K sessions sends roughly 70 to 80 percent
through the hero funnel (testable); 80 SKUs across 6 collections at 50K might send only 8K to
10K to the hero PDP (fragmented). The sweet spot is 1 to 15 SKUs.

**Qualification bands.**

| Tier | Single-product sessions | 2-15 SKU sessions | Monthly revenue | Monthly ad spend |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Foundation | 25,000 to 80,000 | 40,000 to 100,000 | $75K to $400K | $15K to $60K |
| Growth | 60,000 to 200,000 | 80,000 to 250,000 | $300K to $1.2M | $50K to $150K |
| Turnkey | 150,000+ | 200,000+ | $1M+ | $125K+ |

**Overlap zones.** The ranges overlap on purpose. Inside an overlap, need drives the tier, not
just what the brand qualifies for.

- Foundation / Growth overlap: ~60K to 100K sessions, $300K to $400K revenue
- Growth / Turnkey overlap: ~150K to 250K sessions, $1M to $1.2M revenue

Move a brand UP when: they have tested before and want velocity; ad spend is growing
month over month; they want immersive experiences; they lack in-house design or photography
(Turnkey absorbs it); they have multiple angles or segments to test; a major launch or rebrand
is within 6 months. Keep a brand LOWER when: new to a CRO partner; strong in-house execution
team; steady not aggressive spend; one clear hero product; want to start lean and upgrade on
results. Discipline rule: in an overlap zone, ask at least three qualifying questions before
recommending a tier.

**Edge cases.** High revenue with low ad spend is a qualifying conversation, not a
disqualifier (lifts compound across organic too; usually Growth). High AOV ($150+) flexes the
revenue thresholds down. Subscription brands sit at Growth as a floor because LTV raises CRO
ROI. Complex catalog at low sessions is usually a "not yet": simplify the catalog or return at
higher volume.
