# Post-Click Positioning

What post-click is, where it sits in the growth engine, and who it is for.

Post-click is everything after the click. Paid media gets the visitor to the site. Post-click
turns that visitor into a customer. We design, build, and test the landing pages, funnels, and
on-site experiences that convert paid traffic, and we treat it as part of the growth engine,
not a bolt-on service.

Pixel Theory is the strategic growth partner for scaled consumer brands. The agency does not
just run ads, it runs growth: the financial models and cohort analysis that say which customers
are worth acquiring and what to pay for them, then the media, the creative, and the post-click
experience that goes and gets them. Post-click is one layer of that engine. The pre-click side
buys the traffic and makes the creative. We own what happens once the visitor lands. CAC does
not end at the click, and the brands that win treat both halves as one system.

## What post-click is, in one line
The after-the-click layer of the growth engine: the pages, offers, and experiences that turn
paid clicks into revenue, validated by testing rather than opinion.

We call the category **Post-Click Revenue Maximisation** on purpose. "CRO" undersells it and
puts us in a crowded, burned-out bucket. We are not optimising a button colour. We are
engineering the revenue a brand earns per session.

## Where it sits in the growth engine
- **Pre-click owns the route in.** Media buying, channel strategy, creative, the angle and the
  audience. That is the growth-strategy and media side of the pod.
- **Post-click owns what happens after.** The landing page, the PDP and buy box, the offer
  architecture, the subscription flow, the on-site experience. The variable lives on the page
  or in the offer, not in the ad account.
- **It is one engine, not a service queue.** Post-click is a peer layer to media and creative,
  not subordinate to them. The data, the buying, and the page should never be disconnected. A
  validated on-site winner feeds back into what the media team scales, and a working angle from
  media becomes the next thing we test on-site.

What post-click does: iterative, sequenced experiments on the pages that already receive
traffic, working backwards through the funnel from landing pages and PDPs out to homepage,
navigation, and core functionality. The goal is conversion rate, AOV, and LTV together, because
those three are what actually move advertising performance and profit.

What post-click does not do: full brand redesigns, market positioning, or go-to-market
strategy. We touch those only where they directly change how a page converts. We do not claim
sole ownership of a single KPI either. Lifts compound across the whole funnel, so the work is
collaborative by design.

## How we are different
Most CRO work fails the same way: isolated A/B tests based on "best practices", vanity metrics
like raw conversion rate, and Figma files the brand's dev team never ships. Results plateau or
regress inside three months. What we do instead:

- **Research first.** We start with the customer (surveys, often 1,000+ responses) before we
  open a design tool. The brief comes from real buyer psychology, not industry benchmarks.
- **Sequenced, compounding tests.** Each winner becomes the new control. The roadmap evolves
  with the data instead of spraying parallel tests and hoping. (MASA: four sequenced tests,
  +162% revenue per session.)
- **We measure money, not vanity.** Revenue per session, subscription attach rate, LTV. Not
  just CVR.
- **We build into the theme, not into a slide.** Wins ship directly into the brand's Shopify
  build, so they become permanent infrastructure rather than throwaway test pages. No handoff,
  no dev bottleneck.
- **We think in offers, not headlines.** Pricing, bundles, decoys, subscription mechanics,
  shipping thresholds. The offer is usually the biggest lever, and most "CRO" never touches it.

The diagnostic lens behind all of this is **OMAU**: Offer, Motivation, Anxiety, Usability. It
is the proprietary way we read a brand's post-click experience. The mechanics live in
`02-methodology/`, but the name belongs here because it is the reason results compound rather
than plateau.

## Who it is for
Scaled and scaling consumer brands running real paid spend, where there is enough traffic for
testing to resolve and enough revenue for a lift to matter. The narrative read, not the bands
(the qualification bands and the minimum floor live in `01-offering/offering.md`):

- **A focused brand.** A hero product or a tight line, ideally 1 to 15 SKUs. A focused catalog
  concentrates traffic onto the few pages worth testing. A sprawling catalog fragments it, and
  the program crawls. Two brands at the same session count can be worlds apart on testability.
- **Spending on paid, and feeling the page is the bottleneck.** They are buying clicks and the
  page is not converting the way the spend deserves. The answer is rarely more traffic.
- **Past the experiment-cannot-resolve floor.** Below the minimum in offering.md, structured
  testing cannot reach significance inside a sprint, and we steer them to heuristic CRO until
  they are consistently above it.

The buyer shows up in two shapes, and the pitch flexes to match:

- **Earlier-stage founder.** Spending on ads, clicks are landing, conversion is soft and they
  do not know why. They need educating on what post-click even is: offer testing, the buy box,
  subscription mechanics, research-led decisions. Tone is energetic and direct, a mentor to a
  founder. Often this is their first structured CRO partnership.
- **Later-stage operator (VP of E-commerce, CMO).** Has been through two or three CRO agencies
  and been burned. They do not need educating, they need to know why this time is different.
  Tone is measured, authoritative, data-heavy, peer to peer. No profanity, no hype.

The right tier inside that, and the qualifying questions, are owned by the sales director and
documented in `01-offering/offering.md`.

## What we want a brand to feel
Not "we make prettier pages". The vision we sell is the brand's own post-click experience
working as a compounding asset: an optimised buy box, a tested offer, a quiz or listicle in
their own brand styling, revenue per session tracking well above baseline, and a roadmap that
keeps paying out. A growth partner building post-click infrastructure, not a vendor running
one-off tests. The difference between "hire us to run tests" and "here is what your growth
engine looks like" is the whole pitch.
