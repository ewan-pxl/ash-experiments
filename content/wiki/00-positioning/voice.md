# House Voice and Operating Principles

How post-click writes, and the principles the department runs on.

This is the `humanify` house style applied to everything post-click ships: decks, proposals,
SOWs, briefs, client Slack, case studies. The test is simple. It should read like a smart
person typed it fast, not like an AI report or an agency template.

## How we write
- **No em dashes. Anywhere.** Use commas, periods, or parentheses instead.
- **Plain language, concrete over vague.** Say the real thing. "+162% revenue per session"
  beats "significant uplift". Show the number, name the page, name the lever.
- **No buzzwords, no AI slop.** No "leverage synergies", no "unlock", no "in today's
  fast-paced landscape". If a sentence could open any agency deck, cut it.
- **Quote the terms the reader owns.** Use the brand's own words for their product, their
  customer, their offer. Put quotes around terms they own ("buy box", "the four-pack",
  "subscription attach").
- **Read it aloud.** If you would not say it to the founder on a call, rewrite it.
- **Calibrate the tone to the reader.** Earlier-stage founder: energetic, direct, a little
  edge is fine, mentor energy. Later-stage operator who has been burned: measured,
  authoritative, data-heavy, no profanity. Same substance, different framing. (See the buyer
  shapes in `positioning.md`.)

## What we never do in writing
- Open with throat-clearing or a definition the reader already knows.
- Bury the revenue impact. Lead with the dollar number or the lever, not the process.
- Tell a design story when the prospect needs a revenue story. Before/after screenshots prove
  "prettier". We are selling "engineers more revenue".
- Over-format. Bullets and short callouts, not nested headings and tables for their own sake.
  The calling doc decides structure, the voice does not impose one.

## Operating principles
These are how the department thinks, not just how it writes. They show up in the work.

- **Partner, not vendor.** The line we hold internally is the gap between quick vendor-style
  output and genuine revenue-maximising partnership work. We build infrastructure that
  compounds, we do not rent out test execution.
- **Research before design.** No one opens a design tool before the customer research is in.
  The brief comes from buyer psychology, not best-practice guesses.
- **Sequenced, not sprayed.** Tests compound. Each winner becomes the next control. We do not
  run parallel A/B tests and hope something lands.
- **Measure money.** Revenue per session, AOV, subscription attach, LTV. Conversion rate alone
  is a vanity read.
- **Ship into the theme.** Wins go live in the brand's real Shopify build so they stick. We do
  not hand over Figma files and walk away.
- **Own the call.** We read the data and decide what wins, what ships, and what dies. We do not
  push that decision back onto the client.
- **Say it straight, including the bad news.** When a test loses or performance dips, we run the
  hard conversation rather than dressing it up. If a fact is unconfirmed (like a price), we say
  so.
- **Async by default, calls when they matter.** Most communication is Slack. Calls cluster
  around launches, roadmap reviews, and hard conversations. Performance gets handled async and
  shared for review, calls are for direction, not data dumps.

## How the team is built
The department runs on pods and is built to scale without leaning on any one person.

- **Pod model.** Small, senior pods own a book of brands. Roles inside a pod: strategy lead,
  coordinator/strategist, tech/dev, and design. The intent is to scale to multiple pods rather
  than one heroic operator. The wider agency runs the same pod shape on the pre-click side
  (growth strategist, media buyer, creative strategist).
- **Director (Ash).** Owns strategy across every client program, reads the test data and calls
  what wins or dies, runs the hard founder conversations, and builds the department itself:
  pricing, sprint structure, hiring, standards, the sales pitch and positioning. Directs design
  rather than producing it.
- **Strategy (Lucas).** Day-to-day test running, routine data reads, deck and copy drafting.
- **Tech/Dev (Ewan).** All Shopify back-end, dev builds, QA, Intelligems setup.
- **Account/strategist.** Day-to-day client Slack, request triage, holding the testing roadmap.
- **Sales director (Graham).** Owns the sales motion and tier placement (see
  `01-offering/offering.md`).
- **Systems over heroes.** Playbooks, templates, and tooling (Claude Code, Figma) exist so the
  work is repeatable and not bottlenecked on one head.
