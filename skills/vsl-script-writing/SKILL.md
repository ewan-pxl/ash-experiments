# VSL Script Writing

Write million-dollar VSL scripts using battle-tested frameworks from the masters of direct response. A VSL is a long-form sales letter in video form — a read-along video — so we borrow heavily from proven copywriting principles.

## Core Principle

Copy cannot create desire — it channels existing desires toward your product. You must understand your prospect's hopes, dreams, fears, and beliefs BEFORE writing a single word of script.

## Quick Start Workflow

### 1. Discovery & Planning (MANDATORY — Do This First)

You MUST gather answers to the planning questions before writing. Do not skip this step.

See [planning.md](references/planning.md) for the complete discovery questionnaire. At minimum, you need:

- **Product Details**: Name, function, price, discounts, bonuses
- **Right Customer / Wrong Customer**: Who this is for and who it is NOT for
- **Goal, Enemy, Hero**: The transformation arc
- **Audience Frustrations** (at least 7): What keeps them up at night
- **Audience Desires** (at least 7): What they dream about
- **Their Greatest Wish**: The single biggest outcome they want
- **Proof of Concept**: Credibility, demonstrations, fast/easy/safe evidence, regret prevention
- **Reasons Why**: Why buy, why not competitors, why your company, why now, why they might NOT buy (objections)

### 2. Strategic Foundation

Before writing, define:
- **Big Idea**: One intellectually interesting AND emotionally compelling concept that leads to an inevitable purchase conclusion
- **Unique Mechanism**: The proprietary system/method/ingredient that delivers results (give it a name)
- **Primary Promise**: The single biggest benefit
- **Target Awareness Level**: Match your lead type to audience awareness (see [frameworks.md](references/frameworks.md))
- **Enemy**: What force is working against your prospect (a villain, a myth, a hidden problem)

### 3. Structure Selection

Choose your VSL length and framework:
- **Short-Form (Under 10 min)**: Hook → Problem → Solution Tease → Quick Story → Offer Stack → CTA
- **Long-Form (20-45 min)**: Hook → Problem/Agitation → Epiphany Bridge Story → Solution/Mechanism → Offer Stack → Price/Guarantee → Close
- **Agora-Style (45-90 min)**: Fear Hook → Massive Credibility → Deep Agitation → Proof Avalanche → Mechanism Reveal → Offer → Urgency Close

See [frameworks.md](references/frameworks.md) for detailed structural breakdowns.

### 4. Write the VSL Script

The default output is a **written script** — the words that will be spoken in the video. Write it as flowing copy, section by section, following the chosen framework structure. Think of it as a sales letter that will be read aloud.

**The Four-Legged Stool** (every VSL needs all four):
1. **Big/Unifying Idea** — One emotionally compelling concept
2. **Substantial Promise** — Clear benefit with supporting claims
3. **Ample Proof** — Evidence for every claim (see [proof.md](references/proof.md))
4. **Loads of Credibility** — For product, expert, and company

**Rule of One** for blockbuster VSLs:
- One good idea
- One core emotion
- One captivating story
- One single, desirable benefit
- One inevitable response

### 5. Create the Slippery Slide

Every sentence exists to get viewers to the NEXT sentence:
- Short punchy sentences that demand continuation
- Seeds of curiosity: "But here's where it gets interesting..." "Now here's the best part..."
- Open loops that must be closed
- Pattern interrupts to re-engage attention every 2-3 minutes

### 6. Close Construction

- **Future Pace**: Paint a vivid picture of their life after using the product
- **Value Stack**: List all components with individual values (perceived value = 10x price)
- **Price Anchor & Drop**: Show high anchor → reveal actual price → show per-unit or per-day breakdown
- **Guarantee**: Longer guarantee = fewer refunds. Be bold.
- **Urgency/Scarcity**: Real deadlines, limited quantities, or price increases
- **FAQ Section**: Handle remaining objections
- **Take Away Close**: Remind them this isn't for everyone

### 7. (Optional) Convert to Slide-by-Slide Format

If the user requests it, convert the written script into a slide-by-slide production format. See [examples.md](references/examples.md) for slide formatting examples. This includes:
- Breaking copy into `[SLIDE]` markers (1-2 sentences per slide)
- Adding `[VISUAL: description]` cues for images, charts, or demonstrations
- Noting emphasis words with **bold** or (RED TEXT) markers
- Adding `[CTA BAR]` notes where persistent buttons should appear
- Adding `[PAUSE]` markers for dramatic effect

This step is NOT the default. Only do this when explicitly asked.

## Quality Checklist

Before delivery, verify:
- [ ] All planning questions answered before writing began
- [ ] Single Big Idea that leads to inevitable purchase conclusion
- [ ] Four-Legged Stool complete (Idea, Promise, Proof, Credibility)
- [ ] Clear Enemy and Unique Mechanism (with a proprietary name)
- [ ] Lead type matches audience awareness level
- [ ] Hook grabs attention in first 3-10 seconds
- [ ] Epiphany Bridge story creates emotional connection (not a lecture)
- [ ] Three levels of pain addressed (external, internal, philosophical)
- [ ] Golden Thread maintained (emotional tension never goes slack)
- [ ] All claims have supporting proof
- [ ] Value stack perceived value is 10x+ the asking price
- [ ] Clear, urgent CTA with risk reversal
- [ ] Passes CUB test: Not Confusing, Unbelievable, or Boring
- [ ] Read aloud — sounds conversational, not like a lecture
- [ ] (If requested) Script converted to slide-by-slide format with visual cues
